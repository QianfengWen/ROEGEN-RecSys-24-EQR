'''Kalamazoo''' [http://www.ci.kalamazoo.mi.us] is a city in [[Southwest Michigan]].  As of the 2000 census, the city had a total population of 77,145, with a metropolitan area of 452,851, which includes neighboring Battle Creek.  It is the county seat of Kalamazoo County.  

The city has a strong higher education presence, being home to Western Michigan University (with 25,000 students), Kalamazoo College (a private, liberal arts institution), and Kalamazoo Valley Community College.

Because of the the first downtown pedestrian mall in America, Kalamazoo got the  nickname the "Mall City" In 1959"

==Get in==

===By plane===

The Kalamazoo-Battle Creek International Airport serves the area.  Major connections include Chicago (American Airlines), Minneapolis (Delta), Detroit (Delta), Sanford, FL (Direct Air), Punta Gorda, FL (Direct Air).

===By car===

Kalamazoo lies on Interstate 94 roughly mid-way between Chicago and Detroit. US Highway 131 connects the city with [[Grand Rapids]] and resort areas further north.

===By train===

Amtrak offers service to Chicago and Ann Arbor/Detroit several times daily on the ''[[Wolverine Service]]''.  A train ride to Chicago is about 2.5 hours.

===By bus===

The downtown train station also acts as a bus station.  Major destinations include Grand Rapids, Chicago and Detroit.

==Do==
Kalamazoo is full of great events, featuring everything from Hot Rods [http://www.nsra-usa.com/annual.htm] to Pianos [http://www.thegilmore.com/].  Discover Kalamazoo [http://www.discoverkalamazoo.com/calendar_of_events/annual_events.htm] has a fairly comprehensive list of annual happenings, but don't miss Super Saturday [http://www.kiarts.org/museum/art_fair.shtml] in June, New Year's Fest [http://www.newyearsfest.com/] or the USTA Boys National Championship [http://ustaboys.com/] in August.

For a small city, Kalamazoo also offers an interesting and walkable Downtown [http://www.central-city.net/], with one-of-a-kind shops, restaurants and the free Kalamazoo Valley Museum [http://kvm.kvcc.edu/].

Nearby, you will find the Kalamazoo Air Zoo [http://www.airzoo.org/], Binder Park Zoo [http://www.binderparkzoo.org/], and the Gilmore Car Museum [http://www.gilmorecarmuseum.org/].

Kalamazoo enjoys an arts-friendly community, tempered by such establishments as the Arts Council of Greater Kalamazoo [http://www.kalamazooarts.org/home4]and The Gilmore [http://www.thegilmore.com/]. The city hosts an "Art Hop" in the downtown area on the evening of the first Friday of every month. Independent artists, students, and groups or organizations are featured in galleries hosted at several different businesses and venues.

==Stay safe==

Generally safe, Kalamazoo has some crime, mostly in the north- and south side sections of town. Downtown could also be dangerous after dark, stay in a group and be aware. Try to steer clear of the Edison neighborhood on the south side.

==Get out==

The Lake Michigan resort towns of [[South Haven]] and [[Saugatuck-Douglas]] are about an hour west of Kalamazoo.  Gull Lake (NE) and Gun Lake (North) are clear, spring-fed lakes of over 1000 acres each.  Each offers boating and water-sports opportunities.  Gull Lake has sailboat races on weekends.
