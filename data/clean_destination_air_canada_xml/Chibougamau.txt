Chibougamau is the largest regional center in Northern Quebec. First founded as a mining village in 1950, Chibougamau obtained its status of municipal corporation in 1954.

Get in
By plane

By bus
Intercar operates a bus service to Chibougamau. Their busses are available throughout most of the province, including and the Saguenay region.

By car
From southern Québec, the general route to Chibougamau is Route 167 from Lac St-Jean, from where it is possible to travel to via and Roberval on Route 155. Western Québec and Ontario are connected to Chibougamau by Route 113, connecting to Route 117 at Louvicourt near . Both routes are paved with a speed limit of 90 km/h.

The North Road (Route du Nord), an isolated gravel road, connects Chibougamau to the James Bay Road (Route de la Baie-James).

Get around
There is no public transport in Chibougamau. Two taxi services, Taxi Diamond and Taxi Coop, are located on 3e rue.

See
*Cree communities: Chibougamau is the largest regional center to visit the aboriginal Cree communities of Oujé-Bougoumou (47 km west) and Mistissini (86 km north). Oujé-Bougoumou was designed by renowned Canadian architect Douglas Cardinal, who designed the Museum of Civilization in Gatineau, Québec, and has received UNESCO recognition as a model community due to sustainable development practices and cultural preservation.
