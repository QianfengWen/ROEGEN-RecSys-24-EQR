
The village of Woods Hole administratively belongs to the town of Falmouth, in the Upper Cape area of Cape Cod, Massachusetts. Woods Hole has a great natural harbor and was once a fishing and whaling center but now is known for its beautiful beaches, sport fishing, quaint shops (no chain stores here) and marine research facilities.

Get in[edit]
By bus[edit]
Long-distance[edit]
 Peter Pan Bus Lines, toll-free: +1 800-343-9999, +1 888-751-8800. Service from Boston and between Bourne, Falmouth, Barnstable and Hyannis. 
Within Cape Cod[edit]
 Cape Cod Regional Transit Authority. Woods Hole/Falmouth to Hyannis, and other services around the Cape. 
By boat[edit]
 Steamship Authority Ferries, 65 South St, ☏ +1 508-477-8600. Year Round ferry service to and from Martha's Vineyard is offered by the Steamship Authority, including car transport. 
Get around[edit]
On foot[edit]
Woods Hole is a wonderful place to walk around; everything is within 15 minutes' walk for people of normal fitness.

By bicycle[edit]
The Shining Sea bikeway begins at the end of Railroad Ave, right next to the Steamship Terminal & the Leeside Restaurant. It's a great way to get to Woods Hole from downtown Falmouth, about 4 miles, mostly along the ocean.

By trolley[edit]
WHOOSH Trolley[dead link] provides local summer service around Falmouth.

Buy[edit]
Water St.
There are many small shops & galleries in the village, many featuring local artists, all within a 5-minute walk.  There is an ATM at the bank, at the top of Water St., right next to the U.S. Post Office and Pie in the Sky.
