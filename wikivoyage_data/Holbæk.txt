


Holbæk is a city in Zealand. Holbæk is an important harbour city and a commercial and cultural center for the area.


Understand[edit]
Holbæk is the seat of Holbæk Municipality and the second largest city of West Zealand Region with 30,000 inhabitants (2022).  The city is situated by Holbæk Inlet, which in turn is connected to the larger Ise Inlet.  
Holbæk's history dates back to around 1200.  The city expanded from around 1300 when the king built a castle here.  However, the city has historically not been as important as other major cities in the area and only little from the city's early history has survived until today.

Get in[edit]
The nearest airport is Copenhagen, and there is highway all the way from the capital (route 21)
Holbæk is a major stop on the Copenhagen-Kalundborg railway line [1] running nearly in a straight line west over Zealand with departures once or twice per hour during most of the day. It takes around 50 minutes from Copenhagen and Kalundborg, and around 30 minutes from Roskilde. Holbæk station is the interchange to the private Regionstog's[dead link] line to Nykøbing Sjælland, which covers nearly all of the Odsherred Peninsular.
If you are coming from West Denmark change to bus 13 in Slagelse (Intercity trains), it takes around an hour, the bus continues to Nykøbing Sjælland and gets there a bit faster than the train. Another option is catching the ferry [2] to Kalundborg from Aarhus and catch a train from there. Most of the Abildskou intercity buses between Copenhagen and Jutland stops at the Q8 gas station in Tuse (Kalundborgvej 220).
