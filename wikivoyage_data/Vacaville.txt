
Vacaville is in Solano County in the Sacramento Valley of California.


Get in[edit]
By car[edit]
 Vacaville is about 55 mi (89 km) northeast from San Francisco and 34 mi (55 km) southwest of Sacramento. Vacaville is accessed from Exit 51B (Pena Adobe Rd) through Exit 59 (Meridian Rd, Weber Rd) of I-80. Nearest BART station to Vacaville is the Concord Station, 35 mi (56 km) southwest, in Concord (via Concord Ave from Exit 52 of I-680).
 from . I-505 goes 32 mi (51 km) from I-5, near Dunnigan (exit 553), to Exit 56 of I-80 in Vacaville.  This serves as a short cut for travelers coming from Dunnigan or anywhere further north to the San Francisco Bay Area (via Vacaville) without going into Sacramento.

Nearest Greyhound station/stop is Suisun City/Fairfield. They travel along I-80 between San Francisco, Oakland, Vallejo, Suisun City, Sacramento, Colfax, Roseville, Tuckee and/or Reno. By train[edit]
38.2856-121.9683 Amtrak Fairfield/Vacaville (Station ID: FFV), 4921 Vanden Rd (SE of the intersection of Vanden and Peabody Rd, 5.5 mi (8.9 km) south of Vacaville on Peabody Rd). Served by: the Capitol Corridor on its way between San Jose (via Oakland and Emeryville) and Auburn (east of Sacramento). The Capitol Corridor serves as a commuter train and therefore, operates more frequently than the other trains. As this is an unmanned platform with a shelter, tickets (or passes) should be purchased beforehand online or over the phone to be presented to the conductor at boarding. Travelers can transfer to the San Joaquins or the California Zeyphyr trains in Sacramento or to the Coast Starlight in either Sacramento, Oakland or San Jose. Amtrak operates a Thruway bus to San Francisco from Emeryville; from Martinez to Arcata; and from San Jose to Santa Cruz/Monterey.
