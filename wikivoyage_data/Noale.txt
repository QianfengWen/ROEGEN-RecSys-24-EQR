

Noale
Noale (Noàłe o Noàl in Veneto dialect) is a quiet countryside town located between Venice, Treviso, Padova and Castelfranco Veneto.
The churches, buildings and palaces of the town centre testifies to Noale’s important past, both during the Middle-Ages than under the Republic of Venice. The walls, the defensive system, the Medieval Fortress and Castle all remind of a time of lords, duels and parades... a time which is revised every here during the famous Noale Palio.
A relaxing stay in Noale will also give you the opportunities to taste the culinary specialties of the Venice area, with its very interesting combination of seafood and meat. A visit to the local restaurants and trattorias is also an opportunity to taste one of the several dishes which are prepared with the main protagonist of Noale’s cuisine, the duck.
Finally, why not shop at the medieval markets or search for something unique at the Trovarobe flea market on Sunday?

Get in[edit]
By train[edit]
Noale railway station has direct train connections with Venice, Padua and Treviso:
Venice is 40min ride away
Padua is 1h ride away
Treviso is 1h ride away
More info is available on Trenitalia’s website ([1]).

45.553812.07481 Noale-Scorzè railway station (Stazione di Noale-Scorzè).  (updated Mar 2021)
By plane[edit]
Two international airports are conveniently located nearby:

Venezia Marco Polo (VCE) ([2]), 31km, lot of destinations throughout Europe by train: bus to Venice station, then train
Treviso (TSF) ([3]), 19km, low-cost airport with Ryanair and other carriers by train: bus to Treviso station, then train
By car[edit]
Noale is just 35km north-west of Venice, 28km north-east of Padua and 23 km south-east of Treviso. Noale can easily be reached through the A4 Milano-Trieste highway (Martellago/Scorzé exit).

By bus[edit]
ACTV’s line no. 5 ([4]) directly connects Noale to Venice (1h).
