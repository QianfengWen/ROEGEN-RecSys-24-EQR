
Libertyville is a village and township in Far North Suburbs of the Chicagoland region.


Get in[edit]
By car, Libertyville can be reached from the Rockland Road and Buckley Road exits off of I-94. It's about 45 minutes from Chicago and about an hour from Milwaukee.
Libertyville is on the Milwaukee District North Line of the Metra commuter rail system--the Chicago-to-Fox-Lake route. The train ride to or from Chicago takes a little more than an hour.

See[edit]
 Adlai Stevenson Center on Democracy, 25200 N. St. Mary's Rd.. A nonpartisan organization dedicated to promoting democracy housed in the historic home of former Illinois governor and two-time presidential candidate Adlai Stevenson. 
 David Adler Music and Arts Center, 1700 N. Milwaukee Ave. (south of Buckley Rd.), ☏ +1 847-367-0707. This performance and educational center is on the former estate of noted architect David Adler, who lived in and extensively redesigned an 1864 farmhouse that has been preserved much as it was at Adler's death in 1949. 
42.258533-87.9351191 Lloyd Lewis House, 153 Little St Mary's Rd (near the Des Plaines River). A Frank Lloyd Wright house designed in 1939 for the editor of the Chicago Daily News.   (updated Dec 2020)
 St. Sava Serbian Orthodox Monastery, 32377 N. Milwaukee Ave (north of Buckley Road/Route 137), ☏ +1 847-223-5480. The cemetery of this religious institution (which also includes the St. Sava School of Theology) is the resting place of King Peter II of Serbia, the only European monarch to be buried in U.S. soil. 
42.2848-87.9662 Libertyville High School, 708 W Park Ave, ☏ +1 847-327-7000. The town's high school was the alma mater for many of the alt rock figures who emerged improbably from Libertyville's non-scene, notably Tom Morello of Rage Against The Machine and Adam Jones of Tool.   (updated Dec 2020)
Do[edit]
 Canlan Sports Libertyville, 1950 N. Highway 45, ☏ +1 847-350-7806. Athletic center featuring basketball/volleyball courts, indoor soccer fields and a 28-foot "climbing mountain." 
Buy[edit]
Libertyville is well-known for car shopping; many Chicagolanders who know nothing else about the town can tell you that it's "a beautiful place in the country," thanks to a car dealer's long-running jingle. The car dealerships are found in a long strip along Milwaukee Avenue.

 Dover Saddlery, 300 Peterson Rd. (west of Milwaukee Ave.), ☏ +1 847-367-9600. Has one of the largest selection of riding-related gear in the Midwest.

 The Tavern, 519 N Milwaukee Ave, ☏ +1 847-367-5755. Fancy steak place.

Drink[edit]
 Downing's, 431 S. Milwaukee Ave, ☏ +1 847-362-4020. Neighborhood bar with a youngish crowd; the owners are siblings who bought the place with the proceeds from a winning lottery ticket. 
42.291084-87.9574581 Hansa Coffee Roasters, 755 N Milwaukee Ave, ☏ +1-847-226-8815. 6am weekdays, 7am weekends, to 6pm. Right across the tracks from the train station, a repurposed auto shop serving good coffee. Usually has interesting seasonal drinks. On nice days, the garage doors will be opened, filling the shop with fresh air. (updated Apr 2022)
