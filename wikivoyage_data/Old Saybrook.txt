
Old Saybrook is a city of 10,000 people (2020) in Connecticut at the mouth of the Connecticut River. Families and tourists enjoy Old Saybrook's beaches, the high quality of restaurants, and the peaceful and  serene atmosphere. Many people travel from all over in their high class boats which they dock at the marina next to the ocean. Old Saybrook is where the Connecticut River meets Long Island Sound.

Description: The beautiful Lynde Light in Old Saybrook.

Get in[edit]
Old Saybrook is on the west side of the mouth of the Connecticut River, 45 miles from Hartford, 101 miles from New York, and 105 miles from Boston.

By plane[edit]
Groton-New London Airport, 155 Tower Ave., Groton, ☏ +1 860-445-8549. It takes about 30 minutes by car to get from the airport to Old Saybrook.
By train[edit]
Amtrak, 455 Boston Post Road, ☏ +1-800-USA-RAIL (872-7245). The station is open 5:15AM-10PM , Ticket sales end at 9:30PM.
Shore Line East, 455 Boston Post Road (Same Building), ☏ +1-800-ALL-RIDE (255-7433).
By bus[edit]
Estuary Transit District, 455 Boston Post Rd., ☏ +1 860-395-0891.
The buses are called 9 Town Transit Service. Buses that run along the shoreline provide regular service along route 1 from Madison to Old Saybrook and all points in between. Its stops include Madison center, Hammonassett State Park, Clinton Crossings, Tangers Outlets, YMCA, Walmart, and the Old Saybrook Train Station.
The Riverside Shuttle provides regular service along route 154 from Old Saybrook to Essex, Deep River, and Chester. Regular stops will include the Old Saybrook Train station, Essex Square, Adam's Market, and the Center of Chester.
By car[edit]
Old Saybrook is located off Interstate 95 at Exit 68. Old Saybrook is about 1 hour and 15 minutes to 1 hour and 45 minutes from Providence and 2 hours and 15 minutes from New York.

See[edit]
Fort Saybrook Monument Park, Saybrook Point. 18 acres of beautiful woods owned by the Town of Old Saybrook. Storyboards "walk you around" and tell the story. Free.
 General William Hart House, 350 Main Street, ☏ +1 860-388-2622. Mid-Jun to mid-Sep: Sa Su 1-4PM. Representing some of Connecticut's oldest architecture, this house was built in 1767 by a prominent citizen in Old Saybrooks history. Volunteer tour guides will take you around this elegant piece of American history. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places and home to the local historical society, this mansion features eight corner fireplaces. Award-winning historical gardens. $5.
