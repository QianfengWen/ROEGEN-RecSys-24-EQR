
Harwich [dead link] is a town of about 13,000 people (2020) in Massachusetts on Cape Cod. It includes the villages of Harwichport, South Harwich, and West Harwich.


Get in[edit]
Map of Harwich (Massachusetts)

Route 6 to Exit 10, right on Route 124.

Get around[edit]
Freedom Cruise Line, Inc.[dead link], 702 Main St., Harwich Port, ☏ +1 508-432-8999. Ferry service to Nantucket.
Bike the Cape Cod Rail Trail. This 22-mile bike trail follows an old railroad bed through several towns. Check their website or call them to check for closures.
See[edit]
Beaches[edit]
 Bank Street Beach, Bank St (on Nantucket Sound near Harwichport center; at the end of Bank Street). This beach is typically busy in the summer season, and with its warmer waters is a great location for swimming, lounging, and for the kids to chase and catch hermit crabs. (updated May 2018)
 Pleasant Bay Beach (Off Route 28). Calm water beach with sandbars. Popular with clammers. Open to non-residents. (updated May 2018)

Do[edit]
 Bud's Go-Karts, 9 Sisson Rd, ☏ +1 508-432-4964. Kids can ride Go-Karts and hit the ball in the batting cages for about $6 a person. To ride your own Go-Kart you need to be at least 8 years of age and to ride with someone you need to be at least 16 years of age. Adults can ride with children that are younger than 8 for the same price of $6. (updated May 2018)

Buy[edit]
Main Street (Route 28), Harwich Port has a string of interesting shops.

 Harwich Antique Center, 10 Route 28 (Main Street) (just over the Harwich/Dennis line), ☏ +1 508-432-4220, harwichantiquecenter@comcast.net. M-Sa 10AM-5PM, Su 11AM-5PM. Harwich Antique Center is a large group shop featuring antique, primitive and vintage furniture, jewelry, coins, paper items, books, military, trains, art glass, fine china, advertising, musical and nautical items and memorabilia. From $1 to high end items. There are weekly free events on everything from furniture restoration workshops to free appraisals to animal rescue benefit days. (updated May 2018)

 Handkerchief Shoals Inn, 888 Route 28, South Harwich, ☏ +1 508-432-2200, contact@shoalscapecodinn.com. This unpretentious roadside hotel is 6 miles from the 19th-century Chatham Light lighthouse and 9 miles from biking and hiking at Nickerson State Park. The conservatively decorated rooms come with free Wi-Fi, flat-screen TVs and coffeemakers, as well as microwaves and minifridges. Kids age 12 and under stay at no charge. Complimentary amenities include parking and breakfast (seasonal). There's also an outdoor pool. (updated Jun 2018)
