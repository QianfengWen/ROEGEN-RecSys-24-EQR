
Nundle is a village (population 496 in 2016) in the New England region of New South Wales in Australia.


Understand[edit]
Nundle was founded in 1852 when gold was discovered at the nearby Hanging Rock diggings. Nundle has several notable historical buildings, and one can prospect for gold, minerals, and gemstones in the surrounding district. The nearby Hanging Rock Lookout offers impressive views over the Peel River valley.

Get around[edit]
Private transport is essential, as there is no taxi service nor is there a local bus service in Nundle.
The nearest car rental agencies are in Tamworth and Quirindi.
