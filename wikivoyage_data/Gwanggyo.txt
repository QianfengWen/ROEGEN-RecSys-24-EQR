
Gwanggyo is  a modern and bustling city located in the heart of South Korea's Gyeonggi Province. With its blend of cutting-edge architecture, lush green spaces, and a rich cultural heritage, Gwanggyo offers travelers a diverse range of experiences. Whether you're interested in exploring urban landscapes or enjoying serene natural beauty, Gwanggyo has something for everyone. 


Understand[edit]
Gwanggyo, also known as "Gwanggyo New Town," is a planned city in Suwon, just south of Seoul. It was designed with a vision of urban living that incorporates sustainable and eco-friendly principles. The city's modernity is evident in its futuristic architecture, including the iconic Gwanggyo Lake Park Footbridge, and its commitment to green spaces. However, it also preserves South Korea's rich cultural heritage with historical sites and traditional Korean experiences.

Get in[edit]
By air[edit]
The nearest major airport to Gwanggyo is Incheon International Airport (ICN), which is approximately 70 km to the west. From the airport, you can easily reach Gwanggyo by taxi, limousine bus, or KTX train.

By train[edit]
Gwanggyo is well-connected to Seoul by the KTX (Korea Train Express), with frequent services running between Seoul Station and Gwanggyo Station. The journey takes around 30 minutes, making it a convenient option for travelers.

By bus[edit]
Several intercity buses connect Gwanggyo to neighboring cities and regions. The Gwanggyo Bus Terminal serves as a hub for these services, making it easy to access the city by road.

Get around[edit]
37°18′0″N 127°4′12″EMap of Gwanggyo
Gwanggyo has a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy for travelers to explore the city. The following are some of the primary modes of transportation:
Buses: Gwanggyo has an extensive bus network that covers the city and its surrounding areas. Bus routes are well-marked in both Korean and English, making it accessible for tourists.
Taxis: Taxis are readily available and are a convenient way to get around, especially if you prefer door-to-door service. Most taxi drivers understand basic English, but it's helpful to have your destination written in Korean.
Bicycles: Gwanggyo promotes cycling as a sustainable mode of transportation. You can rent bicycles from various locations throughout the city and explore its bike-friendly paths.
Walking: Gwanggyo's city center is designed with pedestrians in mind, featuring pedestrian-friendly streets and well-maintained sidewalks.

Stay safe[edit]
Gwanggyo is generally a safe city for travelers. The local police are helpful, and crime rates are low. However, like any other urban area, it's essential to take standard precautions, such as safeguarding your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.
