
Verőce (previously Nógrádverőce, German: Werowitz, Slovak: Verovce) is a village in the Danube Bend region.


Understand[edit]
47°49′36″N 19°1′57″EMap of Verőce

Its name was first mentioned in a diploma between 1444 and 1460. The settlement was one of the ancient estates of the bishopric of Vác. After the Turkish occupation, the village was rebuilt by Hungarians, Slavs and Germans who returned from Upper Hungary.

By narrow-gauge train[edit]
Mk48 diesel locomotive in Kismaros
Királyrét Forest Railway (Királyréti Erdei Vasút) operates narrow-gauge trains between Kismaros and Királyrét. It was the oldest forest railway in the Börzsöny mountain range. The trains are operated between April 1 and September 30 every day, and between November 1 and March 31 on Saturdays and Sundays. The ticket costs for adult: 500 Ft–900 Ft; for senior, student: 400 Ft–500 Ft (depending on the distance).
