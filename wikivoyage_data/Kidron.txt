
Kidron is a village of 1,100 people (2020) in Wayne County in Northeast Ohio. Kidron is the most popular tourist location for Amish related tourism in Wayne County. The Amish frequent the town as a major location to do business.


Get in[edit]
Map of Kidron
Route 52 - This route runs from north to south through the town. Route 30/ Old Lincoln Highway intersects north off 52 and Route 250 intersects south off 52.
Route 188 - Runs east to west through town intersecting with Apple Creek/Route 250 to the west and Route 94 to the east.

Kidron Auction and Flea Market - Intersection of Kidron Rd.(CR52)and Emerson Rd.(CR79), +1-800-589-9749, Ohio's Oldest Livestock Market, Livestock sale every Thursday at 11AM (except May 25 Ascension Day and Nov.23 Thanksgiving Day, sales held on Friday, May 26 and Nov.24). Flea Market held every Thursday and Saturday, early morning-1PM, seasonal.
 Ohio Mennonite Relief Sale. Held the first Saturday in August, Central Christian School, 3970 Kidron Road, proceeds go to the Mennonite Central Committee fund to assist with humanitarian efforts. Famous quilt auction, also handicrafts from local craftsman and around the world.
