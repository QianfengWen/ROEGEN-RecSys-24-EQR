
Turangi is a town of 3,840 (2023) at the south end of Lake Taupo, in the Central North Island of New Zealand.

Get in[edit]
By bus[edit]
Intercity have three services per day from Auckland and from Wellington.

By car[edit]
Turangi is on State Highway 1, between Taupo and Waiouru.

See[edit]

Do[edit]
 Tongariro River Rafting, 95 Atirau Rd. A variety of rafting experiences, including raft fishing and a blue duck experience, featuring the endangered native whio (blue duck). (updated Jan 2022)
 Tongariro River Trail. Walk or mountain bike. It's 15 km in total, but you can walk just parts of it. You can start it at i-SITE Visitor Information Centre. Tongariro National Trout Centre is on the route. (updated Dec 2018)
Trout fishing. Turangi is mostly famous for the Tongariro River, which runs through it. It provides excellent fishing all year round (but particularly during August-October) for rainbow (steelhead) and brown trout.
Hunting
Walking, climbing and (in season) skiing in nearby Tongariro National Park
