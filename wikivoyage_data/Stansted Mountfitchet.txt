
Stansted Mountfitchet is a village in Essex.


Understand[edit]
Get in[edit]
By car[edit]
Take Junction 8 (A120) of the M11. On the B1383 3 miles north of Bishop's Stortford.

By train[edit]
51.9010.21 Stansted Mountfitchet railway station. Connects to the West Anglia Main Line Cambridge - London Liverpool St, as well as connections to Stansted Airport station.   (updated Oct 2023)
By plane[edit]
51.8850.2352 London Stansted Airport (STN IATA). Is a few miles away on the other side of the M11 motorway.   (updated Oct 2023)

See[edit]
51.90290.20071 Mountfitchet Castle. All-weather heritage entertainment complex in Stansted Mountfitchet. Formerly an Iron Age hillfort, the castle also has a Roman, Saxon and Viking past, with its Norman ringwork and bailey fortification, many houses complete with all the smells of a bygone era, a toy museum, the Living Norman village, and also a tea room & gift shop. As you stroll through the grounds you will encounter plenty of rescued animals roaming freely: deer, goats, geese, peacocks, chickens as well as Jacob Sheep.   (updated Mar 2017)
