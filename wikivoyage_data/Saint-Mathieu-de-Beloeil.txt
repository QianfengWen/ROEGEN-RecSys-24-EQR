
Saint-Mathieu-de-Belœil is a modern municipality, with agriculture, aviation, industry and recreational tourism, in the regional county municipality (MRC) of La Vallée-du-Richelieu, in Montérégie, in Quebec, Canada.
The main attractions of this municipality are, in particular:

its Gilles-Beaudet airport on the runway, located on the south side of Highway 20, generating a lot of aviation-related activity;
its golf clubs, notably the Arpents Verts golf club, and that of the Vallée du Richelieu which straddles Sainte-Julie and Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil;
its industrial park along Highway 20, offering various products and services for recreational tourism activities, including Contant Beloeil (ATV dealer), RV St-Cyr and the Alouette campground;
its sugar shacks, restaurants and dining rooms, offering a variety of dishes;
its cultural cachet, in particular through the creations of its artists and artisans;
its municipal sports and leisure infrastructures, in particular its parks;
its road infrastructures, in particular highway 20, road 229 (linking Varennes and Rougemont).

Get in[edit]
By car[edit]
Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil-Welcome panel at the corner of Trudeau and Bernard-Pilon streets in May 2022
From Montréal (37 km, time: 0 hr 31 min. Take the Jacques-Cartier bridge (south); take route 132 (eastbound); take Highway 20 (east) to the Saint-Jean-Baptiste Street exit; go northwest to the village of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil.
From Quebec (222 km, time: 2 hr 15 min). Take Highway 20 (westbound) to the Saint-Jean-Baptiste Street exit in Saint-Marc-sur-Richelieu (near the Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil airfield); go northeast on rue Saint-Jean-Baptiste to Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil.
By plane[edit]
The municipality of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil owns the airfield infrastructure. However, this aerodrome is administered by the Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil Airport Corporation. The toponymic designation of this aerodrome evokes the merit of the late Gilles Beaudet, mayor of the municipality of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil, from 1997 to 2005. This change of designation was formalized on June 10, 2021 at the Commission de toponymique du Québec.
This aerodrome offers a paved runway open year-round. This airport area offers parking, storage, maintenance and repairs of aircraft. Several aviation businesses are located there. Some aviation companies offer aircraft rentals, air charters and guided tours. In addition, several enthusiasts come to follow their pilot training at one of the training centres established at this airport. In addition, several companies specialize in the field of helicopters.

Business and commerce are mainly centred near Highway 20, which crosses the municipal territory from east to west. Sainte-Julie offers a pedestrian heritage circuit to discover the Old Village under the historical aspect with 26 information panels; a mobile, self-guided version is available on the city's website. This city provides access to several recreational and tourist activities (hiking, cycling, library, eight municipal parks with various leisure facilities, activities for young people, etc.)
45.65-73.36 Saint-Amable A city with a mainly agricultural, residential, industrial and recreational tourism vocation. Located in the middle of the land, between the Richelieu River (to the east) and the St. Lawrence River (to the west), Saint-Amable enjoys accelerated residential development, especially due to its proximity to Montreal, the industrial parks of the region and telecommuting. Recreational tourism activities are varied, including cycling, hiking, camping, sugar shacks, municipal parks, etc.
