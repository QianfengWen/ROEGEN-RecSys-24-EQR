
Xenia (Pronounced: Zeen-ya) is a city in Southwest Ohio, near Dayton.


Understand[edit]
Xenia is the county seat of Greene County Ohio and was founded in 1803. The name stems from the Greek word for hospitality, and is the largest city in the United States whose name begins with the letter X with over 26,000 residents as of 2017.

By bike[edit]
All of the following bike paths connect at Xenia Station, and are part of the Ohio to Erie Trail project.

39.68041-83.930531 Little Miami Bike Trail (Little Miami Scenic Trail). A 50-mile (80-km) trail, which can be used by runners, hikers, bicyclists, and roller bladers. Heads north to Yellow Springs and then to Springfield. At Springfield it connects with the Simon Kenton Trail which goes to Urbana and the Buck Creek Senic Trail which goes to Springfield parks. Going south, the trail passes through Spring Valley, Corwin near Waynesville, Morrow, South Lebanon, the outskirts of Mason, Loveland, Milford, Newtown, and ends at the edge of Cincinnati near the Cincinnati municipal airport.   
39.67998-83.930362 Prairie Grass Trail. Goes northeast to Cedarville and then to South Charleston where it is interrupted by a few blocks of local roads before continuing to London. From London it is possible to connect to the Roberts Pass trail a few blocks away, which eventually merges into the Camp Chase trail and ends in Battelle Darby Creek Metro Park in suburban Columbus. (updated Mar 2019)
39.68025-83.933183 Creekside Trail. Goes north to Beavercreek, then splits north to the Mad River Recreation Trail and the downtown core of Dayton, and south to the Iron Horse Trail which leads to suburban Dayton.  (updated Mar 2019)
39.6788-83.92664 Jamestown Connector Bikeway. Connects to Jamestown Ohio, and from there goes on to Rosemoor, which is close to the intersection of I71, US35, and SR435 where there are some shopping outlets. (updated Mar 2019)

See[edit]
In Xenia[edit]
39.6803-83.93131 Xenia Station, 150 Miami Avenue. 1998 replica of Xenia's 1880s brick railroad station. Now the hub for a number of rail trails. Has car parking, restrooms, a preserved railroad caboose, and a splash pad.   
39.6856-83.92892 Greene County Courthouse. A historic courthouse with a large clocktower.   (updated Dec 2018)
39.6411-83.88333 McDonald Farm. A historic farm that was once provided the highest quality limestone in the state. The farm helped provide stone for the Washington Monument in Washington, DC.   (updated Mar 2019)
39.6647-83.92444 Ohio Soldiers' and Sailors' Orphans' Home. A historic orphanage built to help heal the wounded after the American Civil War, the orphanage only ceased to function in 1997. It now  houses Christian ministries such as Athletes in Action.   (updated Mar 2019)
In Wilberforce[edit]
Wilberforce is a small community next to Xenia. Named after William Wilberforce who helped abolish the slave trade in the British empire, this community was an important stop on the Underground Railroad.

39.7153-83.8785 Central State University (CSU), 1400 Brush Row Rd, ☏ +1 937-376-6011, toll-free: +1-800-388-2781. A historically black public university across the street from Wilberforce University.   (updated Mar 2019)
39.7175-83.8819446 Wilberforce University, 1055 N Bickett Rd, ☏ +1 937-376-2911. A historically black private college that was the first to be owned and operated by African Americans. W.E.B. Du Bois was once a professor here.   (updated Mar 2019)
39.7131-83.87697 National Afro-American Museum and Cultural Center (NAAMCC), 1350 Brush Row Rd, ☏ +1 937 376-4944, toll-free: +1 800 752-2603, InfoNAAMCC@ohiohistory.org.   (updated Mar 2019)
39.70818-83.893298 Charles Young Buffalo Soldiers National Monument, 1120 US Route 42 E, ☏ +1 937-352-6757. By appointment only. The preserved home of a former slave who became the third African American to graduate from West Point, and the first African American to reach the status of Colonel in the American army. Free.   (updated Mar 2019)
