
Sargans is a municipality in the canton of Saint Gallen in Eastern Switzerland.

By train[edit]
Sargans is an important regional hub. Trains heading north toward St. Gallen, east toward Chur and Ticino, and toward Austria, all pass through Sargans. Trains are operated by Swiss Federal Railways.
The train connections from Zurich to Sargans are very good; there is a train every 30 minutes.

By car[edit]
Sargans can be reached via the A3 motorway (from Zurich) or the A13 motorway (from St. Margrethen).

The tours are mostly for organised groups, but a list of public tours for individuals to join is usually posted on the website. You will experience the mysterious world of the miners. Do[edit]
In and around Sargans, you can do many different sports.

 Climbing. You can climb in a hall or outdoor. Pilatus is a particularly popular climbing region, offering a range of routes in all degrees of difficulty. 
Snow sports. Flumserberg, the Pizol, and Amden are popular places for snow sports. In winter, there is an ice park in Sargans [1].
47.13379.362131 Sichelchamm. An alpine hike trail (difficulty around T4) leads to the top of this mountain from the nearby dwelling 'Verachta'. The ledge of rock forms a striking fold in the western flank of the Sichelchamm. The mountain owes its name to these crescent-shaped parallel arches.
