
Kaub is a town in the Middle Rhine Valley, in the  state Rhineland-Palatinate.


Understand[edit]
Beim Alten Zollhaus, one of many cultural heritage monuments in Kaub
Get in[edit]
By Car[edit]
On the B42 north of Rüdesheim
Fährgemeinschaft Kaub Ferry across the Rhine from the B9, Bacharach / Oberwesel.

By cruise boat[edit]
KD[dead link] (Köln Düsseldorfer Deutsche Rheinschiffahrt AG)  and BRFS [formerly dead link]  (Bingen-Rüdesheimer Fahrgastschiffahrt) boats cruise the Rhine stopping at Kaub.

By train[edit]

See[edit]
Burg Pfalzgrafenstein with the boat that ferries between Kaub and the toll station in the background
50.0831487.7654761 Pfalzgrafenstein Castle (Burg Pfalzgrafenstein). Toll station on the Rhine island built in 1327.   
50.08647.76382 Blücherdenkmal (Monument to Blücher). On New Year's Night in 1813/1814 the Prussian General Blücher and his 60,000 soldiers with 20,000 horses crossed the Rhine with the aid of the pilots from Kaub and a pontoon bridge, to drive Napoleon out of Germany. 
50.0879057.7660513 Burg Gutenfels. 13th-century castle above the town. Visitors can walk round the exterior, but is in private hands.
