Get around[edit]
See[edit]
-27.936916153.18971 Tamborine Mountain Glow Worm Caves, Cedar Creek Estate, 104-144 Hartley Rd, ☏ +61 7 5545 1666. Daily 10AM–4PM. An artificial glow worm sanctuary that consists of two large chambers interlinked by tunnels. The first chamber is the "presentation cave", where you will be shown an audiovisual display on glow-worms and the construction of the caves. This chamber is complete with very realistic formations (speleothems), such as stalagmites and stalactites, water features and flow stone. (updated Jun 2022)
-27.860278153.1783332 Tamborine National Park. A national park that has lots of short walks that randomly emerge out of the town. It was Queensland’s first national park declared in 1908, features the famous “Witches Falls”, which only requires you to do a 1.5-km walk. Walking tracks are provided in six sections of Tamborine National Park. Most walking tracks are short and can be walked within an hour or so. The walks are relatively easy although some tracks have short slightly steeper sections. Free.   (updated Jun 2022)
-27.913033153.1879423 Tamborine Rainforest Skywalk, 333 Geissmann Dr, ☏ +61 7 5545 2222. Daily 9:30AM–5PM. Take a short stroll along this 30-metre tall steel bridge in a tranquil rainforest setting. It's not a very long walk, but there's more to see along the way, including lookouts and some impressive bridges. There is also an eco-centre next to the carpark, which has some great information about Subtropical rainforests. (updated Jun 2022)
Do[edit]
 Landcare's Environmental Park, Licuala Drive North Tamborine, opposite number 56. More information at the Piccabeen Bookshop, near Forest Cafe, Main Street North Tamborine or at the Visitors Information Center, Doughty Park, North Tamborine. (updated Oct 2018)

The environmental activities of the local community come together in a very active local Landcare group. The group runs a successful website and the mountain's only second hand bookshop 'Piccabeen' (proceeds go to the rainforest preservation). The Esme Street Environmental Park offers a rainforest experience without the need to go 'off the beaten track'. The flat footpaths are sealed and wheelchair accessible. Signs provide information about flora and fauna. The footpaths add up to only a few hundred meters in length.
Hop On Hop Off - All Day Mountain Explorer, +61 7 5545 4321
All day Mountain Explorer runs from 10AM to 4PM daily. There are over 15 stops on this tram style trolley. Adult, concession & family passes available.
