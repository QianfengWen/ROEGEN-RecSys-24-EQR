
Mount Vernon is the county seat of Knox County, Ohio. It has a small, cute downtown, and is home to Mount Vernon Nazarene University and the manufacturer Ariel.


Understand[edit]
History[edit]
Founded in 1805, Mount Vernon was named after George Washington's Plantation in Virginia.

By car[edit]
The easiest way into Mount Vernon is by car. Mount Vernon is connected by US36.

By bus[edit]
 Knox Area Transit (KAT), (office) 25 Columbus Road, Mt. Vernon, ☏ +1 740 392-7433, kat@co.knox.oh.us. M–F 6AM–7PM, Sa 6AM–5PM. Door-to-door service in and out of Knox County (call for rates), as well as $1 shuttles between Mount Vernon and Gambier (hourly M-F 8AM-10PM, Sa 2-10PM) and within Mount Vernon (see below). 
40.3845-82.48162 Go Bus (Operated by Barons Bus), (bus stop) Colonial City Lanes (Bowling Alley) 110 Mt Vernon Ave (Bus stop at front parking lot. Bowling alley does not sell tickets or offer parking for bus riders.), toll-free: +1-888-95 -OBUS (6287). Offers regular service between Columbus, Newark OH, Martinsburg, Harrison Township, Gambier, Mt Vernon, Loudonville, Shreve and Wooster. 
By bike[edit]
The Kokosing Gap Trail connects to Gambier and is part of the Ohio to Erie Trail from Cincinnati to Cleveland via Columbus.

Get around[edit]
By bus[edit]
In addition to service to and from Mount Vernon, Knox Area Transit offers shuttle service within the city on four lines: Blue (North), Red (East), Orange (South), and Green (West). Shuttles cost $1 and run every half hour M-Sa 8AM-4PM. Transfers are available to the shuttles to Gambier.
