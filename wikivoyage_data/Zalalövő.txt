
Zalalövő is a town in Zala County in the valley of Zala river.


Understand[edit]
46°50′47″N 16°35′6″EMap of Zalalövő

The Roman legions created a military camp on the northern shore of the Zala river at the beginning of the 1st century. Around the camp a village-like settlement founded. In 124, Emperor Hadrian gave town status for the settlement, and called as Municipium Aelium Salla. However, in the middle of the 2nd century the invading Germanic tribes destroyed the settlement.
According to a 13th-century document, royal archers lived here. Zalalövő was elevated to town status in 2000.
This article includes travel-related information not only for Zalalövő, but also for the surrounding rural areas.

Get in[edit]
By train[edit]
Direct trains are available once a day from Budapest-Déli pályaudvar via Székesfehérvár, Veszprém and Zalaegerszeg. The journey takes about 4 hours and costs 4430 Ft.
Other direct connections are:

Toward east: Zalaegerszeg seven times a day. The journey takes about 30 minutes and costs 465 Ft.
Toward west: Őriszentpéter seven times a day. The journey takes about 15 minutes and costs 310 Ft. There are direct international trains via Bajánsenye to Hodoš (Slovenia) four times a day, and to Ljubljana once a day.
46.845616.57741 Zalalövő Train Station, Táncsics Mihály utca 10, ☏ +36 1 349-4949, informacio@mav-start.hu. There is no ticket office, tickets can be purchased on the train. The train station is just 700 m (2,300 ft) away from the center.  (updated Dec 2018)

By bus[edit]
Frequent buses are available from Zalaegerszeg. The journey takes about 30 minutes and costs 465 Ft. From Budapest-Népliget, you have to transfer in Zalaegerszeg. The whole journey from Budapest takes 4 hours and costs 4770 Ft.

Get around[edit]
There is no local public transport or taxi service. You can use the regional buses operated by Volánbusz.
