
Moorhead is a city in Minnesota of about 35,000 which is closely associated with its partner city, Fargo, North Dakota. Moorhead is a vibrant college town with two exceptional universities - Concordia College (private) and Minnesota State University (public) - in addition to a regional vocational school, Minnesota State Community and Technical College. Moorhead is sometimes referred to as the "twin city" of the larger Fargo, which is its neighbor across the Red River in North Dakota. Together Moorhead and Fargo are the hub of the Fargo-Moorhead Metropolitan Area, with suburbs Dilworth, West Fargo, Horace, and Harwood. Within this metropolitan area one finds college and professional sports, hundreds of restaurants, extensive and varied shopping opportunities, a surprising concentration of youth-oriented nightlife, several auto racing venues, and a great range of lodging choices.


Get in[edit]
Map of Moorhead


By car[edit]
Moorhead is at Minnesota's western border on Interstate Highway 94.  US Highway 10 has its western terminus in Moorhead, and US Highway 75 passes through the city in a north/south orientation.
By train[edit]
The Fargo/Moorhead area has rail service via Amtrak (the Empire Builder), with the passenger terminal at 420 4th Street North in Fargo. Trains only come once a day in each direction, in the middle of the night.
By plane[edit]
Two airports serve Fargo/Moorhead:
Hector International Airport (FAR IATA), a regional commercial airport, is in northern Fargo,  ☏ +1 701 241-8168. 
Moorhead Municipal Airport, several miles east of Moorhead on Interstate 94,  ☏ +1 218 287-1400. 
By bus[edit]
Bus service is provided by Greyhound Bus Lines, 402 Northern Pacific Ave N, +1 701 293-1222.
Get around[edit]
Matbus provides local bus service around the Fargo/Moorhead area six days a week.
