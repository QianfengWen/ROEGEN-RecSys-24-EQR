
Lake Country is in the Okanagan region of British Columbia. It is a collective name for administrative and travel purposes given to the communities of Winfield, Oyama, Okanagan Centre and Carr's Landing.


Get in[edit]
Lake near Okanagan Centre
Kelowna International Airport is located about 10 minutes south of Lake Country by car along Highway 97.

Do[edit]
Shores of Kalamalka Lake near Oyama
Wine tasting.  Lake Country is at the north end of the Okanagan's vineyard and winery region.  See the "Drink" section for local wineries.
50.03043-119.400731 Creekside Theater, 10241 Bottom Wood Lake Rd, ☏ +1 250-766-9309. Offers lots of local, and non-local acts including music, plays, and films. Located just off Highway 97N (Berry Road Intersection) on Bottom Wood Lake Road. It's easy to find, and is a pleasant place to sit back and enjoy some local talent. 
Various hiking trails in the area - Drive up Beaver Lake Road, and there are a few trails along the way. A popular one for hikers/atv/dirtbikers is The High Rim Trail - which runs from Vernon, through Lake Country to Kelowna. WALC (walk around lake country) has created a map of walking trails.
 Art Walk. Held in September. A show for local artists to show off their talent. Always a really interesting thing to go and see - you can also buy pieces of art from the artists. There are things for children to do, and there is live music throughout most of the two days. 
50.09053-119.347042 Oyama Zipline, 5617 Oyama Lake Rd, ☏ +1 250-548-4129, toll-free: +1-888-947-2869. Awesome three-hour course with 3,900 ft (1,200 m) of zip lines.  Operates seasonally. (updated Mar 2023)
Lots of opportunites to do water sports in the summer - you can choose from one of the many lakes in the area, and test your skills at wakeboarding, water skiing, or just dragging the kids behind the boat in tubes.
50.0281-119.40023 Swalwell Park. Includes a skatepark for all those skateboarder/bmx lovers out there, Trethewey Spray Park for all those children looking for relief from the heat, a baseball diamond, soccer field, and various sculptures in the area. Open seasonally. (updated Mar 2023)
Various sport areas - soccer, baseball, basketball. Every park located in Lake Country has a soccer field. 50.05055-119.397214 Reiswig Park (Located on Woodsdale Road) is the most popular park for soccer. It also has a beach area, as well as a trail that leads to 50.05077-119.398945 Beasley Park(which also has beach access) that is right beside it. Reiswig park also has picnic tables that can be used for families to picnic at. 50.03633-119.434286 Jack Seaton Park has walking trails, baseball diamonds, and a tennis court available for the public to use.
Many different beaches to swim at. As already mentioned, there is Reiswig park, and Beasley Park (located adjacent to each other on Woodsdale Road)
There are also beaches in OK Center (Follow Oceola Road down). There is also a 50.04027-119.449857 boat launch in OK Center - with enough room to park your vehicle and trailer there. Watch out though, the road down there is small and winds a lot, and a lot of traffic builds up down there in the summer.

Eat[edit]
There is a variety of choices food wise in Lake Country, it just depends on how much time you have, and what you're in the mood for.

Pubs[edit]
50.02538-119.405911 Woody's Pub, 9882 Highway 97 (Pollard Rd intersection), ☏ +1 250-766-2310. Woody's has a good food menu, and a good drink selection. It's a pub that allows smoking, but you hardly notice it outside of the smoking area, as it's really well vented. There is also a patio you can sit on when the weather is nice. They have drink specials, and food specials every day - and it's a really good deal, you get a lot for how much you pay. Try the Sunday's Steak Sandwich special - it comes with fries, caesar salad, and a steak onto of garlic bread - it's really good. The staff there is really friendly, and seem to always come to offer you more drinks right when your glass is empty. They also have fun things like karaoke nights, live bands that play, sports events and other events that take place throughout the month. It gets really busy in there on weekend nights, but you can still always manage to find a table! (updated Mar 2023)
50.05283-119.410112 Turtle Bay Marina Pub, 2850 Woodsdale Rd (Highway 97 and Woodsdale Rd), ☏ +1 250-766-4534. Turtle Bay is right beside Wood Lake - their patio actually looks over it. It is a family restaurant now, but they are still a pub. They have really good food, and good drink specials, and their staff is friendly as well. You cannot smoke inside the building, but you can smoke outside - not sure if you can smoke on the patio or not. Their Wing special, which is on Wednesday, is always busy as the wings are really good. If you like hot wings, go here and order their hottest - they will burn your mouth! Also, while you're there, try their Turtle Trax - which are criss-cross fries topped with nacho toppings - so good. (updated Mar 2023)
Sit-down restaurants[edit]
50.02949-119.403043 L'Isola Bella, 3250 Berry Rd, ☏ +1 250-766-7625. Italian restaurant (updated Mar 2023)
50.0489-119.404224 The Greek House, 3159 Woodsdale Rd, ☏ +1 250-766-0367. Serves Greek food - very good Greek food. It's a family owned business, run by a very nice couple. Their staff is friendly, and take the time out to chat with you about the food, and are always there when you need something. They also serve pitas with tzatziki dip. They also have a lot of local wines that you can have with your meal, along with a variety of other beverages. The atmosphere is friendly and inviting, and they also have a patio that you can be seated on when the weather is nice. If you are planning to go here, call ahead and make reservations - as they are always full of people! (updated Mar 2023)
50.00264-119.404425 The Jammery, 8038 Hwy 97 North, ☏ +1 250-766-1139. The Jammery serves breakfast, lunch and afternoon tea - here they serve a good variety of food made with BC fruit. There is also an area where you can watch them make jam, and can taste the jam in their gift shop. They also serve it along with their food - it's very good jam! Everyone raves about their breakfast waffles - they are huge! So, if you like waffles, and freshly made jam - you should check this place out!  The Jammery is actually just south of Lake Country's city limits and actually within Kelowna's rural northern end. (updated Mar 2023)
50.05277-119.441946 The Lookout Restaurant, 1055 Camp Rd (inside Gray Monk Estate Winery), ☏ +1 250-999-4722. Inside the winery is this covered patio restaurant. The staff is friendly, and the menu has a variety of different choices. Also, it has a beautiful view, so you can't beat that! Call ahead and make a reservation, otherwise you won't get a table! (updated Mar 2023)
50.02851-119.404027 Wooden Nickel Cafe, 10051 Hwy 97, ☏ +1-604-766-5550. This place is absolutely amazing. It serves breakfast and lunch, but is closed for dinner. They have really excellent food, at good prices -their breakfast is excellent. The staff there is friendly, and always tend to your needs. The inside isn't very decorated, but don't let that throw you off - the pictures they have on their walls tell a lot about the history of the area -it's really neat to go around and look at them all. If you're in the mood for a good breakfast of eggs, toast and whatever else your heart desires - try going here! (Closed on Tuesdays.) The Wooden Nickel is open for dinner service on Thursday, Friday & Saturday nights until 8PM. (updated Mar 2023)
50.0546-119.41038 Winfield Chinese Restaurant, 8-11852 Highway 97 (within Lakewood Mall), ☏ +1 250-766-4171. They have good ginger beef. (updated Mar 2023)
