
Susa (Piedmont) is a town and comune in Piedmont located officially in the  Metropolitan City of Turin . 


Understand[edit]
Susa is a town steeped in history. Capital of the Roman province of Alpes Cottiae, Susa was historically an important stop along the trade routes connecting Italy and France. Today, it is close enough to Turin to be considered a part of the metropolitan area, with easy train access to the city. 

Get in[edit]
Map of Susa (Piedmont)
By rail[edit]
45.1364147.0555311 Susa Railway Station. Located on the Turin-Modane rail line, Susa is easily accessed by train from Turin in just a bit over an hour for about €7 each way. (updated Jun 2024)
By car[edit]
 Autostrada A32. Main highway linking Turin with France. The drive from central Turin to Susa is about one hour and requires the payment of one toll. (updated Jun 2024)
Get around[edit]
On foot[edit]
Susa is small enough that you can generally see the city on foot with little trouble.
