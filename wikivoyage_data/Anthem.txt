
Anthem is a large unincorporated community, built by Del Webb homes, in Greater Phoenix, Arizona. It is usually known as Anthem, Arizona.


Get in[edit]
Map of Anthem
Interstate 17, one of the Phoenix area's main thoroughfares, runs through Anthem. Anthem is about 30 minutes from downtown Phoenix. If coming from the north, Anthem is about 1 hour south of Prescott.
The nearest major airport is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, about 30 minutes from Anthem.
Public transit options into Anthem are limited. The nearest bus station, North Phoenix Metro Center, is about a 15-20 minute drive away, as is the northern terminus of the Valley area's light rail at Metro Parkway.

Get around[edit]
You'll probably opt to get around by driving, but most streets have sidewalks and bike lanes.

Monument honoring members of the US Armed Forces, with five pillars representing the five branches of the US military. The community hosts ceremonies each year on Memorial Day (late May) and Veterans Day (November 11th). Free. (updated Apr 2024)

33.860678-112.1366761 Anthem Community Park, 41703 N. Gavilan Peak Parkway. 6:00 AM - 10:00 PM. This large park includes a number of family-friendly activities and amenities, including playgrounds, fishing holes, a splash pad, a skate park, basketball and volleyball courts, softball fields, and a walking trail. As a suburban development, Anthem's restaurant options primarily consist of local and national chains.
