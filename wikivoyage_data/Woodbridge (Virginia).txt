
Woodbridge is a town in Prince William County and suburb of Washington, D.C., best known for the Potomac Mills shopping mall.


Understand[edit]
While many people visit Woodbridge to shop at Potomac Mills, the historic town of Occaquan is a lovely day trip and there are several great restaurants too.

Get in[edit]
Map of Woodbridge (Virginia)
By car[edit]
Woodbridge is located just off I-95, in the Washington D.C. - Richmond corridor, with the main drag along US-1.

By train[edit]
38.659103-77.2482731 Amtrak train station, 1040 Express Way, toll-free: +1 800 872-7245. The Woodbridge Amtrak train station is served by the Northeast Regional train, which travels north to Washington, D.C., Baltimore, Philadelphia, New York City, and Boston, and travels south to Richmond, and either Williamsburg or Norfolk. There are 3 trains per day. The journey to Washington, D.C., takes 35 minutes and costs $21 if not purchased well in advance. The Woodbridge Amtrak station is located near Route 1, and it is not easy to leave the station on foot. (updated Aug 2021)
By bus[edit]
Potomac and Rappahannock Transportation Commission operates bus services to/from Washington, DC.
38.658882-77.2477322 Greyhound Station, 1040 Express Dr. (updated Aug 2021)
To/from Potomac Mills Shopping Mall[edit]
For more information on riding the Metrorail and using a SmarTrip card, see Washington DC#Get_In.

Prince William Metro Direct Circular operates between Potomac Mills and the Franconia-Springfield metro station. The trip from the Franconia-Springfield metro takes 30 minutes and costs $3.60 if paid using a SmarTrip card. The bus operates every day except Sunday.
Dale City-Washington OmniRide operates between Potomac Mills and the White House and the Pentagon (Bus Bay L1). The bus operates only on weekdays and costs $5.75 if paid using a SmarTrip card. The first buses from Washington leave in the early afternoon, but the journey to Potomac Mills takes only 45-50 minutes, which is faster than taking the Prince William Metro Direct Circular.

Eat[edit]
Woodbridge is home to one of the D.C. area's only two Chicano communities and is a great place to get some Mexican food.

38.658718-77.2506591 Dixie Bones, 13440 Occoquan Rd, ☏ +1 703 492-2205. Daily 11AM-9PM. The best restaurant in Woodbridge. Their barbecue meats are absolutely the best, and their sides are excellent too. Make sure you go hungry. (updated Aug 2021)
38.650487-77.3141862 Arepas Capitol, 14091 Noblewood Plaza, Dale City, ☏ +1 571 402-7297. F Sa 11AM-9:30PM, Su Tu-Th 11AM-8PM, closed M. Venezuelan cafe. (updated Aug 2021)
38.674849-77.2601983 Bistro L'Hermitage, 12724 Occaquan Rd, ☏ +1 703 499-9550. Tu-F 4–11PM; Sa Su 11:30AM–2:30PM, 4–11PM, closed M. French restaurant. Dress up and splurge! (updated Aug 2021)
38.676778-77.2807164 Layla's Lebanese Restaurant, 2217 Old Bridge Rd (Tacket's Mill), ☏ +1 703 492-1500. M-Sa 11AM-8PM  closed Sun. Chicken shwarma, kibbe, chicken kabob, and other tasty Lebanese food. (updated Aug 2021)
38.649918-77.2585595 Ricos Tacos Moya, 14087 Jefferson Davis Hwy (Marumsco Shopping Plaza), ☏ +1 703 499-8602. M-W 10AM-10PM, Th-Su 10AM-midnight. Disproportionately Mexican clientele and only one server who really speaks English (the daughter), and some incredible food. Highlights are the tacos themselves and the menudo, which is not available every day. They also serve the rare-to-D.C. micheladas—beer with spices, salt rim, and often tabasco sauce and clam juice (clamada). They're a lot of fun.

38.575684-77.3144747 Taquizas Virginia, Route 1 and Dumfries Rd, Dumfries (234 Commuter Lot). Authentic Mexican street tacos. (updated Aug 2021)
