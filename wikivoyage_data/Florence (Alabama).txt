
Florence is a city in northwest Alabama near Mississippi and Tennessee.


Get in[edit]
By car[edit]
US Highway 72 which runs from East-West from Chattanooga to Memphis passes through Florence. Most major attractions, hotels,restaurants, etc are located on or near US 72 or Florence Blvd as its known within the City of Florence. US Highway 43 runs North-South from Nashville, TN to Mobile Alabama. It travels the same path as US 72 through Florence.
Florence lies about 48 miles west of Interstate 65 Exit 351 in Athens, Exits 340A-340B Decatur/Huntsville, Exit 310 at Cullman.

By plane[edit]
Near Florence, there are a few options for flying in and out. Boutique Air has service to Northwest Alabama Regional Airport from Atlanta. More flights can be found leaving from Huntsville, AL; Birmingham, AL; Nashville,TN; and Memphis, TN. Huntsville would be the shortest drive at just over one hour. The others are a little more than two hours drive.

Museums[edit]
34.793031-87.6805361 Frank Lloyd Wright Rosenbaum House Museum, 601 Riverview Dr, ☏ +1 256 718-5050. Tu-Sa 10AM-4PM, Su 1PM-4PM. A house built in 1939 and designed by Frank Lloyd Wright. The house is open for tours, which last about 45 minutes. $8 adults, $5 seniors and students.   (updated Nov 2016)
34.790314-87.6699272 The Florence Indian Mound & Museum, 1028 S Court St, ☏ +1 256 760-6427. Tu-Sa 10AM-4PM. The Tennessee Valley area's largest domiciliary mound. Features typical workmanship of the Indians who lived in this area before the Cherokees, Chickasaws, and Creeks. (updated Nov 2016)
34.804013-87.6755433 The Kennedy-Douglass Center for the Arts, 217 E Tuscaloosa St, ☏ +1 256 760-6379. M-F 9AM-4PM. Showcases artists from around the Southeast, and offers classes and workshops to people of all ages. It is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Free. (updated Nov 2016)
34.805053-87.6770134 Pope's Tavern, 203 Hermitage Dr, ☏ +1 256 760-6439. Tu-Sa 10AM-4PM. This building served as a hospital along the way of many Civil War soldiers from both the North and South. It also served time as a stagecoach stop, a tavern, and an inn. The museum houses Civil War artifacts as well as antiques from the 18th and 19th centuries. This remains one of Florence's oldest standing structures. (updated Nov 2016)
 Children's Museum of the Shoals. Contains exhibits displaying the history, people, and events that make up the Shoals' history. The museum is designed to promote learning in a hands-on environment. The museum offers educational workshops year-round for children of all ages. (updated Nov 2016)
34.845739-87.7256245 The Forks of Cypress. This was a cotton plantation located in Florence. The remains can still be seen in the form of 24 Greek columns and the Jackson Family cemetery.   (updated Nov 2016)
Other Sights[edit]
34.800833-87.6258336 Wilson Dam. This 110x600 feet Dam is part of the TVA system. The maximum lift is 100 feet, it is the highest single lift lock east of the Rockies.   
 Wichahpi Commemorative Stone Wall, 13890 Lauderdale County 8, ☏ +1 256-648-0582. Daily 8AM-4PM. Trail of Tears memorial, built by a descendant of one who made the journey. 
 Sacred Way Sanctuary, 4409 Co Rd 200, ☏ +1 256-648-0582. Sanctuary for Indigenous Native American horses. 
Do[edit]
Enjoy fishing at the "Small Mouth Bass Capital of the World" around Wilson Dam, a regular on popular fishing shows. Alternatively, hiking, motor boating, and water skiing can be tried.

Music History Tours[edit]
 Muscle Shoals Sound Studios, 3614 N Jackson Hwy, ☏ +1 256-978-5151. Tu-Sa 10AM-4PM. Visit an important site of musical history in the US where notorieties like Bob Dylan and The Rolling Stones recorded albums, as was popularized in the film Muscle Shoals. 
 Fame Recording Studios, 603 Avalon Ave, Muscle Shoals, ☏ +1 256-381-0801. M-F 9AM-6PM, Sa 10AM-2PM. Another very eventful recording studio for the development of rock and blues American musical styles of the 20th Century, where such likenesses as Aretha Franklin debuted. Take a tour. $10 or varies. 
Festivals[edit]
 W.C. Handy Festival. Enjoy jazz and blues as Florence celebrates its native son. 
 Florence Renaissance Faire, Wilson Park (Downtown Florence). fourth Saturday and Sunday in October. Held in Wilson Park this unusual event draws 30,000 – 40,000 people each year to its site in downtown Florence, Alabama. It is always held on the fourth Saturday and Sunday in October, taking advantage of the fall weather and foliage. Wilson Park hosts a variety of vendors, events, and demonstrations with a medevile theme. Free. 
 The Sam Phillips Music Celebration (started January 2005. It is an annual week long event that takes place the first week in January by celebrated with events that include the Sam Phillips Birthday Party), ☏ +1 256-764-8121. 
 The George Lindsey/UNA Film Festival (is named in honor of Florence native, George Lindsey Lindsey is an actor who is most famous portrayal of Goober Pyle on The Andy Griffith Show. Lindsey is a UNA - known then as Florence State College - graduate.).  
 Arts Alive (began in 1986 and celebrates the art of Southern artist who gather to display their work in Wilson Park). 
 The Spirit of Freedom Celebration (showcases music, and fireworks to celebrate Independence Day on the banks of the Tennessee River). 
 Trail of Tears Commemorative Motorcycle Ride. Commemorates the final point at which area Cherokee Native Americans were shipped off to Oklahoma and the mid-west by the Indian Removal Act of 1830. 
 First Fridays in Florence (arts and music event that occurs every First Friday from April through December in Historic Downtown Florence.).
