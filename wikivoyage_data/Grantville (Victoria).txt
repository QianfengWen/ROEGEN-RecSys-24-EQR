
Grantville is a town of 830 people (2016) on the Bass Highway in the Bass Coast region of Victoria. It is a popular stopping point for visitors on their way to or from Phillip Island, located about halfway between the island and the edge of Melbourne.


Understand[edit]
Get in[edit]
Grantville is located on the M420 Bass Highway, which is easily accessible from Melbourne, as the M420 route forks off from the M1 Princes Freeway at Dandenong. V/Line operates coaches from Melbourne, Dandenong and Koo Wee Rup to Grantville daily.

Get around[edit]
Grantville is walkable, and there's no public transport in town. However, if you wish to access nearby Corinella, where accommodation is available, Westernport Roadlines offers regional bus services from the -38.40817145.530081 Grantville-Glen Alvie Rd/Bass Hwy bus stop to Corinella and Coronet Bay, which takes about 15 minutes to get to Corinella.

A popular animal park where visitors can pat, feed and interact with a variety of native Australian animals, including koalas, kangaroos and wallabies, dingoes, emus, parrots and wombats. During weekdays, you can enter into the koala enclosure where there's a keeper talk every hour on the hour, in addition to a reptile presentation at 1:30pm. Aside from the animals, the park also plays host to a mini-golf course and a bistro. $20 adults, $11 children (4-15), $14 seniors, $54 families (2A+2C); Mini-golf extra. (updated Nov 2015)
