
Snellville is a city of 20,000 people (2019) in the Metro Atlanta region of Georgia, roughly 35–40 minutes east of Downtown Atlanta.


Understand[edit]
Get in[edit]
By car[edit]
From Atlanta: Stone Mountain Freeway and Highway 78
From North: Exit onto GA Highway 20 from I-85 or exit onto Pleasant Hill Road and follow to end of Ronald Reagan Parkway
From South: Exit from I-20 to GA 124
By bus[edit]
An express bus, GA Xpress Route 419 runs a commuter route between Downtown Snellville and major points in Downtown Atlanta. Runs Monday–Friday (closed holidays), with service from Snellville to downtown Atlanta in the morning and from Atlanta to Snellville in the afternoon.

Get around[edit]
33°51′42″N 84°1′29″WMap of Snellville
Snellville has several roads leading into the city center.

US Highway 78 provides direct access to the college town of Athens, Stone Mountain Park, and Downtown Atlanta
GA Highway 124 gives direct access to the city of Lawrenceville and the Mall at Stonecrest
Snellville has no public transportation aside from the GA Xpress bus to and from Atlanta.
