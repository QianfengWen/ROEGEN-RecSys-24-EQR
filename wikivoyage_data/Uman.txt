
Uman (Ukrainian: Умань) is a city in Central Ukraine.


Understand[edit]
Uman was founded in the 17th century. It is a small industrial and historic city, which occupies 50th place in terms of population in Ukraine and the second in the region, one of the major tourist centers of Ukraine, as well as Jewish religious movements - Hasidism.
Besides that Uman features a beautiful and popular botanical park.

Get in[edit]
Uman is 210 km from Kyiv and 273 km from Odesa.

By train[edit]
 Uman Railway station (Железнодорожный вокзал Умань), Zaliznychna str, ☏ +380 474423230. 
From Kharkiv and Poltava: train # 613 Kharkiv — Uman. 
From Donetsk: train Yasynuvata — Uman.

By marshrutka[edit]
48.7611530.219461 Bus station (Уманская автостанция), Kyivs'ka str.1, ☏ +380 474432129, +380 474436245. 
Regular comfortable minibuses (marshrutkas) from Kyiv, Cherkasy, Odesa. In Kyiv it is close from central railway station and central bus station (Moskovska ploshcha). You should seek minibus (Mercedes or Volkswagen) with Ukrainian tablet "Умань".

See[edit]
The entrance to the burial place of Rabbi Nachman of Breslov
 Rabbi Nachman of Breslov burial place (Bielinskoho str.). Rabbi Nachman of Breslov was the Rebbe of the Breslov group of Hasidic Jews. Rabbi Nachman spent the end of his life in Uman town, and requested to be buried there. During Rosh Hashanah (the Jewish New Year) there is a major pilgrimage by Breslov Hasidim and others to visit the grave in Uman.
