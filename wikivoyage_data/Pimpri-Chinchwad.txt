
Pimpri-Chinchwad is a satellite city of Pune in Maharashtra, India. It is known for its automotive, IT and manufacturing industries and most of the IT professionals prefer to stay here. While the city has few things to offer to a traveller, there are some hidden gems around Pimpri-Chinchwad to be explored by travellers.


Get in[edit]
By plane[edit]
Pune Airport (PNQ IATA) is close to Pimpri-Chinchwad.

By train[edit]
Pimpri-Chinchwad is served by five railway stations. Both long-distance and suburban trains stop at least one of these stations.

See and do[edit]
18.677173.89661 Alandi. The resting place of the 13th century Marathi saint Sant Dnyaneshwar. His devotees believe that he is still alive. A temple complex was built at Dnyaneshwar's samadhi and is visited by pilgrims, especially those of the Varkari sect.   (updated Jun 2023)
18.6358373.822772 Annasaheb Magar PCMC Stadium. A multipurpose stadium owned and managed by the Pimpri-Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC).   (updated Jun 2023)
18.69225173.7040563 Ghorawadi Caves (Ghoradeshwar Caves, Shelarwadi Caves). A cave with carvings and statues of Buddhist and Hindu deities.   (updated Jun 2023)
18.73605673.7140834 Indori Fort (Induri Fort; इन्दुरी किल्ला). A fort built by Sarsenapati Khandojirao Yesajirao Dabhade in 1720-21. The fort also has a mint building inside the fort which is in shambles. The entrance gate is in good condition. The fort has 9 bastions which are well connected by a fortification wall. The walls of the fort are 30–40 ft (9.1–12.2 m) high made of basalt rock with brickwork at the top. There is a temple of Kadjai inside the fort.   (updated Jun 2023)
18.67444473.7063895 Maharashtra Cricket Association Stadium (MCA Stadium), Gahunje. It hosts both one-day internationals and test matches with a seating capacity of 37,000 spectators. It serves as the home stadium for the Maharashtra cricket team.   (updated Jun 2023)
 PCMC Hockey Stadium (Major Dhyan Chand Stadium). A field hockey stadium.   (updated Jun 2023)
