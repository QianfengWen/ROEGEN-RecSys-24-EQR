
Amadora is a city in Greater Lisbon, Estremadura, Portugal.


Understand[edit]
Parque Central in Amadora.

Amadora (/ɐ.mɐ.ˈdo.ɾɐ/, uh-muh-DOH-ruh) is one of Portugal's smallest municipalities with an area of 23.79 km2 (9.19 sq mi). It has 175,136 residents (2011), making it the most densely populated in the country. Although lacking the sights and excitement of Lisbon, this bedroom community with excellent transport links to the capital makes a good budget-friendly base to stay at.

Get in[edit]
By metro[edit]
Amadora has three stations on the northwest end of the Blue Line (Linha Azul) of the Lisbon Metro (Metropolitano de Lisboa):

38.7531-9.22421 Reboleira.   (updated Jun 2021)
38.7578-9.21812 Amadora Este.   (updated Jun 2021)
38.7603-9.2053 Alfornelos.   (updated Jun 2021)
By train[edit]
Comboios de Portugal serves three stations in Amadora on the commuter Sintra Line (Linha de Sintra):

38.7599-9.236054 Amadora.   (updated Jun 2021)
38.751-9.2235 Reboleira.   (updated Jun 2021)
38.7465-9.21256 Santa Cruz–Damaia.  (updated Jun 2021)
By bus[edit]
Vimeca / Lisboa Transportes provides service from Sintra and Oeiras and within Amadora itself.
Carris and Rodoviária de Lisboa provide service from Lisbon, Odivelas, and Loures.
By car[edit]
Amadora is served by four motorways:

A9 (CREL, Circular Regional Exterior de Lisboa)
A16 (Circular Exterior da Área Metropolitana de Lisboa)
IC17 (CRIL, Circular Regional Interior de Lisboa, a/k/a A36)
IC19 (Radial de Sintra, a/k/a A37).
