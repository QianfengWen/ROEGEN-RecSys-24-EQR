
Van Buren is a village in Hancock County in Northwest Ohio. Located next to I-75, it is primarily visited as either a brief stop for travelers on the interstate, or as a destination for those visiting the large state park located there.


Understand[edit]
Van Buren was founded in 1833 and was named after the then vice president Martin Van Buren, who would later go on to become the 8th President.

Get in[edit]

By car[edit]
I-75 runs along Van Buren North of Findlay and south of North Baltimore

By bike[edit]
41.18292-83.674891 Slippery Elm Trail. A trail that goes from North Baltimore to Bowling Green. The trailhead in North Baltimore is 5 mi (8.0 km) north from Van Buren on flat country roads.   (updated Mar 2019)
Get around[edit]
As a small village, Van Buren is easy to get around with a car, bike, or on foot.

See[edit]
41.13867-83.649521 Village Square. The historic village square is easily walkable, and has some quaint architecture. Free. (updated Oct 2018)
Do[edit]
41.1333-83.6451 Van Buren State Park, 12259 Township Road 218. A large public park surrounding a lake with hiking and horse trails, as well as camping sites. Boating and fishing is allowed on the lake with a licence. Bow hunting is permitted with a licence. Free.   (updated Jun 2021)
41.1422-83.63332 Allen Township Sports Plex, 12829 State Route 613. Has open baseball diamonds and soccer fields for recreation. (updated Sep 2019)
41.1356-83.64833 Black Knights, 217 S Main St, ☏ +1 419 299-3384. Van Buren Schools has a variety of sports teams that play as the Black Knights. Sports include Football, Baseball, Soccer, and Basketball.   (updated Dec 2019)
 Van Buren Lions Club Apple Butter Festival (Apple Butter Festival). Early October. A festival of apple butter, foodstuff from food trucks, crafts, and music. (updated Oct 2018)
Buy[edit]
As a small village, Van Buren does not have many regularly open shopping opportunities.

41.13685-83.660421 Pilot Convenience Store. Has some chain food outlets and basic travel goods. (updated Sep 2019)
Eat[edit]
A picnic site at Van Buren State Park.
41.173842-83.6475251 Iron Skillet (Petro), 12906 Deshler Rd, North Baltimore, ☏ +1 419 257-3744. In a travel plaza off of I75. It's not fancy but can provide a filling meal on a road trip. (updated Feb 2017)
Drink[edit]
41.13821-83.649261 Dragon's Lair (New York Pizza & Pub), 109 S Main St, ☏ +1 419 299-3620. Typical thin crust pizza place, with a wide variety of toppings. (updated Sep 2020)
Sleep[edit]
41.1349-83.63721 Van Buren State Park. The park offers a few different lodging options. There's a regular camp, an equestrian camp with special amenities for horses, and handicap accessible cabins. (updated Dec 2019)
Connect[edit]
The Findlay-Hancock County Public Library bookmobile stops in town every Monday from 4:45PM-6:00PM.

41.13856-83.649911 US Post Office, 100 S Main St, ☏ +1-419-299-3397. 24/7. A tiny post office in downtown Van Buren. (updated Jun 2021)

Go next[edit]
 Findlay (Travel south on I-75, or take main street south.). The closest city to Van Buren, and the biggest city in Hancock county. (updated Jul 2017)
 Bowling Green (Travel north on I-75). The next closest city, the county seat of the nearby Wood County. Easily reachable by car on I75, or by bicycle on the nearby Slippery Elm Trail. (updated Jul 2017)
 Bluffton. A small college town south of Findlay. (updated Mar 2019)
