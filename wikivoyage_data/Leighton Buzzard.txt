
Leighton Buzzard is a town in Bedfordshire, England.


Understand[edit]
Leighton Buzzard, All Saints Church
Leighton Buzzard is probably best known for the Grand Union Canal and the Great Train Robbery (1963) which took place just outside the town. Nowadays, it is considered a commuter town with good transport links to London and Milton Keynes.

Get in[edit]
By road[edit]
The town lies 5 miles from the A5 main London to Holyhead main road.
The town is bypassed by the A4146 Milton Keynes to Hemel Hempstead main road.
The A418 connects the town to Aylesbury
By train[edit]
51.916311-0.6771 Railway Station, Station Approach, Leighton Buzzard LU7 2LY. On the West Coast Main Line, trains run into London Euston station and Bletchley & Milton Keynes. Station managed and services operated by London Northwestern Railway.   (updated May 2017)
By bus[edit]
Arriva service 150 and RedRose 100 runs between the town and Aylesbury and services F70 & F77 connects to Luton, whilst routes 150 & F70 run to Milton Keynes.

By air[edit]
Luton Airport is 18 mi (29 km) east of the town.

By bicycle[edit]
Leighton Buzzard is on National Cycle Route 6 running from London to the Lake District.

Leighton Buzzard Narrow Gauge Railway
51.9086-0.6517562 BuzzRail (Narrow Gauge Railway), Page's Park Station, Billington Rd, Leighton Buzzard LU7 4TG, ☏ +44 1525 373888. A 3-mile (5-km), former industrial railway that was laid in 1919 to transport sand. Today it is run as a heritage line offering trips for visitors from Page's Park to Stonehenge Works and back in 85 minutes. The line runs mostly through modern housing built since the 1970s, although the last half mile runs through countryside. It is one of the last surviving 2-ft gauge railways. (The standard gauge in the UK is 4 ft 8½ in. The Page's Park station and two coaches are accessible for people who use wheelchairs.   (updated Apr 2020)
