
Mashpee is in Massachusetts on Cape Cod. It includes the village of New Seabury. Purchased in 1660 to be a reservation for the Wampanoag tribe and later revoked and incorporated as a town. Until the 1960s, Wampanoags made up the majority of the population.


Get in[edit]
From Boston take Route 3 south to the Sagamore Bridge and continue on Route 6 to Route 130 which leads to Mashpee.
From Providence take Interstate Route 195 to 495 and cross the Bourne Bridge. Proceed on Route 28 to Route 151.

Parks[edit]
41.6677-70.47642 Lowell Holly Reservation (Off South Sandwich Rd), ☏ +1 508 679-2115. This 135-acre preserve has large stands of holly and Mashpee-Wakeby pond is one of the best freshwater fishing sites on the Cape.   (updated Jun 2018)
41.558436-70.50243 South Cape Beach State Park, 668 Great Oak Rd, ☏ +1 508 457-0495. Restrooms. Excellent beach.   (updated Jun 2018)
41.5815-70.52514 Waquoit Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve (Rte. 28, Entrance in E. Falmouth), ☏ +1 508-457-0495, fax: +1 617-727-5537, Waquoit.Bay@state.ma.us. Wonderful 2200-acre reserve perfect for hiking. Also runs public events such as Evenings on the Bluff and the Watershed Block Party.   (updated Jun 2018)
 Wakeby Pond, Ryder Conservation Area, John Ewer Rd (South Sandwich). Lifeguards, food, and rest rooms. The Cape's largest freshwater body of water. Parking $10.   (updated Jun 2018)
