Paju-Si is known as a historical city with a rich background of past events, even some dating back to the ancient era. Today, Paju-Si is a growing city with the population of almost 500,000 (2022). It is quite close to the DMZ, which is on the border with North Korea.

Get in[edit]


By car[edit]
Paju is a 90-minute drive from Seoul. There are car rentals available in Korea (for instance at the Incheon International Airport and Seoul), where cars can be rented for an unlimited time.

By bus[edit]
From the Hapjeong Station, take bus No. 2200. The bus should take passengers to Odusan Unification Observatory, where passengers should get off at the entry. The first bus leaves Seoul at 07:50, while the last bus leaves at 23:00 for Gyeonggi. This bus ride will take 45 minutes, and will bring passengers straight to Gyeonggi, where Paju city is.

By Taxi[edit]
You can take a taxi at the taxi stand. The taxi stand is usually near the bus stop.
As of July 2023, the basic fare for regular taxis is ₩4,800 (₩5,800 at night), with a surcharge of ₩100 applied according to time and distance. (The basic fare is up to 1.6 km (1 mi), plus ₩100 per 135 m.)
By train[edit]
From Seoul, take the Gyeongui line to Geuchom Station - the ride takes about one hour. From there, take a local bus, either number 2 or 3. The bus ride should only take 30 minutes. The trip altogether should take ninety minutes. The train and bus rides will take you straight to Gyeonggi, where Paju city is.

By plane[edit]
South Korea's main airport, Incheon International Airport, is close to Paju.

Get around[edit]
Although there are no restrictions on visits to the city of Paju, access to the areas to the north towards the DMZ may be restricted.

Reservations can be made online, with a bonus of a discount.

Do[edit]
 Pyeong Hwa Land. A children's theme park that is 100 meters from the DMZ. It boasts 18 rides and 2 million visitors a year who can have fun and learn about the Korean war, which is an unusual combination. 
37.965019126.8896711 Paju Hwangpo Sailboat, Duji-ri, Jeokseong-Myeon, 216-5, ☏ +82 31-958-2557. Sailboats were the mode of transportation during the Chosun Dynasty. Today, tourists will have the opportunity to get on the Hwangpo sailboat to enjoy the scenery from the Imjin River Dojiri to Jajangri. The 40-minute boat ride costs ₩8,000. (updated Sep 2023)

Buy[edit]
 [dead link] Paju Premium Outlets. The outlet is beautifully designed, with a European feel to it with the fountain and clock tower. It’s recommended to visit to enjoy the beautiful view, especially at night. The outlets is also a great place for shopping with a total of 165 stores. Some include International brands such as Jil Sanders, Tory Burch, Levi, Armani, Calvin Klein and local brands like Time, Mine and Buckaro. Since it’s an outlet, shoppers can easily find sales up to  80 percent off. 
Eat[edit]
Paju-Si is full of restaurants for the residents, visitors and travelers. The restaurants vary from traditional Korean to Japanese, Chinese and even some famous Western food chains such as McDonalds.

 Hao Binh (호아빈). Vietnamese restaurant specializing in Pho noodle soup with beef, but offering other dishes such as fried rice and noodle soups as well. The restaurant also offers vegetarian options. 
 Joy’s (조이스). It is recognized a national chain of family Korean restaurants whose specialties include meals with ribs, chicken and steaks.
