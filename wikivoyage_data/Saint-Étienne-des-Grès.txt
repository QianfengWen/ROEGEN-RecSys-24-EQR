
Saint-Étienne-des-Grès is a parish municipality of the Maskinongé Regional county municipality (MRC de Maskinongé), in Basse-Mauricie.
Its geographical location between Shawinigan and Trois-Rivières, along the Saint-Maurice River and its forest environment, provide a pleasant living environment for residents and seasonal visitors. La Gabelle hydroelectric power station and its nature park are popular tourist attractions.


Understand[edit]
History[edit]
The municipality of the parish of Saint-Étienne-des-Grès was established in 1859.

Geography[edit]
This municipality is located on the west bank of the Saint-Maurice River, between the Shawinigan-Sud sector of the city of Shawinigan and the Forges sector of the city of Trois-Rivières. Three exits from Highway 55 give access to the territory of Saint-Étienne-des-Grès, the main one of which links the main village. Given its privileged geographical location, Saint-Étienne-des-Grès is a residential environment close to employment centres and urban services.

Get in[edit]
The village of Saint-Étienne-des-Grès is accessible by train, road, snowmobile and all-terrain vehicles.

By car[edit]
From Montreal (151 km (94 mi), 1 hr 34 min). Take highway 40 (north shore) eastbound, to Trois-Rivières; take highway 55 (northbound); take the exit of the village of Saint-Étienne-des-Grès.
From Quebec City (km|152}}, 1 hr 35 min). Take highway 40 (direction West), to Trois-Rivières; take highway 55 (northbound) to the village of Saint-Étienne-des-Grès.

Heritage tour[edit]
46.449-72.7341 Parc nature La Gabelle, chemin de la Gabelle, Saint-Étienne-des-Grès (access by Saint-Étienne des Grès, via the Gabelle road; access via Notre-Dame-de-Mont-Carmel, via the rang des Grès), ☏ +1 866-388-1978. La Gabelle Park allows you to practice sport fishing, or to indulge in walking on hiking and cross-country skiing trails in winter. This park has several art structures scattered throughout its territory. A belvedere allows a panoramic view of the Saint-Maurice valley and the La Gabelle power station. This park has several picnic areas to appreciate the wild nature. The entrance to the trail is located on the Notre-Dame-du-Mont-Carmel side. A wooden bridge leads walkers to a very large staircase which leads to more than 10 km of trails. The La Gabelle power station offers an inter-connection link open to road vehicles. (updated Nov 2021)
